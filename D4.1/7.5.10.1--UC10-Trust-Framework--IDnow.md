# 9. Trust Framework

## 9.1 Applicable Legal and Regulatory Framework

UC 10 operates within a multi-layered trust framework, combining EU-level regulation with sector-specific and national requirements.

| UC Phase | Applicable Framework | Key Requirements |
|----------|----------------------|------------------|
| **Phase 1 — PID Issuance** | eIDAS 2.0 (Reg. 2024/1183), CIR 2024/2977 | PID issued at LoA High by a notified eID scheme; mandatory and optional attribute set per CIR |
| **Phase 2 — EAA Issuance** | eIDAS 2.0 Art. 45f, OIDC4VCI specification | EAA issued by a QTSP; SD-JWT VC format; pre-authorised code flow |
| **Phase 3 — Verification (booking/check-in)** | GDPR Art. 5 (data minimisation), Art. 6 (lawfulness), OID4VP specification | Selective disclosure of only required attributes; user consent before data sharing |
| **Phase 4 — Room key presentation** | GDPR Art. 6(1)(b) (contractual necessity) | Credential presented via proximity protocol; no PID disclosure at door |
| **Phase 5 — Authority reporting** | EU Reg. 2025/12 (API Regulation), ICAO Annex 9, national hotel registration laws | Minimised PID subset shared with authorities; user consent required (with fallback for refusal) |

## 9.2 ARF Role Mapping

The following table maps UC 10 actors to their roles within the Architecture Reference Framework (ARF):

| Actor | ARF Role | Responsibilities in UC 10 |
|-------|----------|---------------------------|
| **User (Traveller)** | Wallet User | Holds PID and EAAs in EUDIW; consents to data sharing; authenticates at each presentation |
| **EUDIW** | Wallet Instance | Stores credentials; constructs Verifiable Presentations; enforces user consent; maintains transaction log |
| **IDnow** | QTSP / EAA Issuer / KYC Provider | Issues three EAAs (Booking Confirmation, Room Key, Guest Summary); performs PID-based identity verification at check-in; signs credentials with qualified electronic seal |
| **Booking Platform (Participant 1)** | Relying Party | Requests PID verification at booking; triggers EAA issuance via IDnow; validates VP |
| **Hotel (Participant 2)** | Relying Party | Requests PID + EAA verification at check-in; triggers Room Key and Guest Summary issuance; validates VP at door locks |
| **PID Issuer (Government)** | PID Provider | Issues PID to EUDIW at LoA High; maintains PID TIL entry |
| **Authorities (Border / Local Registry)** | Relying Party (public sector) | Receives minimised guest data for regulatory compliance; validates VP |
| **WP2 Testbed** | Trust Infrastructure Provider | Maintains TILs, provides interoperability testing, hosts pilot trust registry |

## 9.3 Trust Chain

The trust chain in UC 10 flows as follows:

- **Root of trust:** EU Member State eID schemes, notified under eIDAS 2.0, issuing PIDs at LoA High.
- **Identity verification:** IDnow verifies the user's PID at check-in, inheriting the LoA High assurance from the PID issuer.
- **EAA issuance:** IDnow issues EAAs based on verified PID identity + hotel business data. EAAs are signed with IDnow's qualified electronic seal, registered in the TIL.
- **Credential verification:** RPs validate credential signatures against the TIL, verify key binding, and check expiry. Trust in the credential is derived from trust in the issuer (IDnow) and trust in the PID (Member State).

## 9.4 APTITUDE Pilot Governance

UC 10 operates within the APTITUDE pilot trust framework, which provides:

- A pilot TIL maintained by WP2, containing registered PID issuers and EAA issuers for the pilot.
- A WP2 testbed for conformance and interoperability testing of wallet-to-RP and wallet-to-issuer interactions.
- Agreed credential formats (SD-JWT VC and mdoc) and presentation protocols (OID4VP, OIDC4VCI) per the ARF implementation profiles.
- Cross-WP coordination points: WP3 for Digital Travel Credentials (not directly used in UC 10 but relevant for Scenario 1 UCs that may share travellers with UC 10), and WP6 for payment components (out of scope for UC 10).

> **IDnow's acceptance:** IDnow accepts the APTITUDE consortium's pilot trust model for the scope of the UC 10 pilot. Production deployment beyond the pilot would require IDnow's full notification as a QTSP under eIDAS 2.0 in the relevant Member State(s), and integration with production-grade TILs maintained by national authorities.

## 9.5 Liability Considerations

Under eIDAS 2.0, the following liability allocation applies to UC 10:

- **PID Issuer liability:** The Member State is liable for the accuracy and validity of the PID.
- **EAA Issuer liability (IDnow):** As QTSP, IDnow is liable for the integrity and authenticity of the EAAs it issues. IDnow is not liable for the accuracy of the underlying hotel business data (room assignments, booking details), which is provided by the hotel.
- **Hotel liability:** The hotel is responsible for the accuracy of booking and room assignment data provided to IDnow for EAA issuance.
- **Wallet Provider liability:** The EUDIW provider is liable for the secure storage and presentation of credentials within the wallet.
