# 7. Electronic Attestations of Attributes (EAA / QEAA)

## 7.1 EAA Classification

All three attestations in UC 10 are classified as **EAA** (not QEAA). The rationale for this classification is as follows:

| Criterion | QEAA Required? | UC 10 Position |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Legal assurance equivalent to an official certificate | Yes for QEAA | Not applicable — a digital room key and booking confirmation are commercial artefacts, not official government documents |
| Relying party needs guarantees from an authoritative source | Yes for QEAA | The hotel is the authoritative source of the room key; IDnow signs as QTSP but the data originates from the hotel, not from a public registry |
| Access to regulated or high-risk services | Yes for QEAA | Hotel check-in is not a regulated high-risk service (unlike banking or eGovernment) |
| eIDAS 2.0 Art. 45f requirements | Yes for QEAA | QEAA issuance requires the attestation to be based on an authentic source that is a public-sector body or a designated private-sector body. Hotels are neither. |

> **Conclusion: EAA classification is sufficient and appropriate for UC 10.**</br>
> Elevating to QEAA would impose requirements (authentic source designation, enhanced liability regime) that are disproportionate to the use case's risk profile.

## 7.2 EAA Structured Entries

The following table summarises each EAA in the format expected by D4.1:

| Property | Booking Confirmation EAA | Digital Room Key EAA | Guest Summary EAA |
|----------|--------------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Issuer** | IDnow (QTSP) | IDnow (QTSP) | IDnow (QTSP) |
| **Format** | SD-JWT VC | SD-JWT VC | SD-JWT VC |
| **Data type** | Dynamic (per booking) | Dynamic (per check-in) | Dynamic (per check-in) |
| **Validity** | Until checkout date | Check-in to checkout | Duration of stay |
| **Selective disclosure** | Yes (see Section 5.2) | No (all attributes needed) | Yes (`guest_preferences` optional) |
| **QEAA required** | No (see Section 7.1) | No (see Section 7.1) | No (see Section 7.1) |
| **Revocation** | No (short-lived) | No (short-lived) | No (short-lived) |
| **Presentation channel** | Online (OID4VP) | Proximity (NFC/BLE) | Online (OID4VP) |

## 7.3 Relationship Between EAAs

The three EAAs have a logical dependency chain:

- **Booking Confirmation EAA** is issued first (Phase 2) and serves as the prerequisite for check-in.
- **Digital Room Key EAA + Guest Summary EAA** are issued together (Phase 4) only after the hotel has validated the Booking Confirmation EAA and the PID.

The EAAs are linked via the `booking_id` attribute, which appears in both the Booking Confirmation EAA and the Guest Summary EAA. The Digital Room Key EAA does not contain a `booking_id` (it only contains room access information), but it is issued in the same transaction as the Guest Summary EAA, establishing a temporal and transactional link.

## 7.4 Attestation Rulebook

Per ARF Section 5.4 and TS11 Section 4, a dedicated attestation rulebook is required for each EAA type. The rulebook defines the credential schema, issuance policies, trust requirements, and presentation rules.

> **Status:** The attestation rulebook for UC 10's three EAAs has not yet been drafted. It is planned as a **D4.2 deliverable**, building on the preliminary schemas defined in Section 2.6 of this document.
