# 3. Verification

## 3.1 Verification Overview

Verification in UC 10 occurs at four points, using OID4VP as the presentation protocol. The verification model differs between online (booking) and proximity (hotel) scenarios.

| Phase | Verification Type | Channel | ARF Flow | Credential(s) Verified |
|-------|-------------------|---------|----------|------------------------|
| **Phase 2 — Booking** | Remote | Online (browser/app) | Remote Same-Device or Remote Cross-Device | PID |
| **Phase 3 — Check-in** | Proximity | In-person (NFC / QR) | Proximity Unsupervised | PID + Booking Confirmation EAA |
| **Phase 4 — Room access** | Proximity | In-person (NFC / BLE) | Proximity Unsupervised | Digital Room Key EAA |
| **Phase 5 — Authority** | Remote | Wallet-to-authority API | Remote Same-Device | PID s


## 3.2 Verification Steps (Proximity Flow — Phase 3/4)

The following steps are performed by the Relying Party (hotel system or door reader) when verifying a Verifiable Presentation:
- Step 1 — Session initiation: The RP generates an OID4VP presentation request containing the requested credential types and attributes. This is encoded as a QR code displayed at the check-in kiosk or broadcast via NFC/BLE at the door lock.
- Step 2 — Wallet response: The EUDIW scans the QR code or detects the NFC/BLE signal, prompts the user for consent (see Section 1), and constructs the Verifiable Presentation.
- Step 3 — SD-JWT VC signature validation: The RP validates the issuer's signature on the SD-JWT VC against IDnow's public key, retrieved from the Trusted Issuer List (see Section 8).
- Step 4 — Key binding validation: The RP verifies the key binding JWT to confirm that the presenter holds the private key corresponding to the cnf claim in the credential.
- Step 5 — Expiry check: The RP checks the exp claim to confirm the credential is still valid.
- Step 6 — Business logic: For Phase 3, the RP matches the guest name from the PID against the booking record. For Phase 4, the RP sends the lock_mac_key to the door lock controller to unlock the door.

## 3.3 Relying Party Registration

Within the APTITUDE pilot, Relying Parties (Booking Platform and Hotel) are pre-registered in the pilot trust environment. Each RP holds an RP authentication certificate that the EUDIW can optionally validate before sharing data.
For Phase 4 (door unlock), the HLR specifies that the EUDIW should share the door key credential even if RP authentication fails (see HLR Section: Presentation & User Interactions, item g). This design choice prioritises guest convenience for the door unlock scenario; a failed RP authentication at the door should not lock the guest out of their room.

## 3.4 Offline Verification

Offline verification is a requirement for UC 10, particularly for Phase 4 (door unlock) where network connectivity at the door lock cannot be guaranteed.
Mechanism: The door reader pre-caches IDnow's issuer public key (or certificate) from the Trusted Issuer List during initial provisioning or periodic sync. At presentation time, the reader validates the SD-JWT VC signature locally using the cached key material, checks the expiry timestamp against its local clock, and verifies the key binding JWT. No network call to IDnow or the TIL is required at the moment of presentation.
- Clock synchronisation: The door reader must maintain accurate time (NTP sync during periodic online windows) to validate expiry claims reliably.
- Key rotation: If IDnow rotates its signing key, updated key material must be distributed to door readers during their next online sync window.

## 3.5 Cross-Border Verification

UC 10 supports cross-border scenarios where a traveller from one EU Member State checks into a hotel in another Member State. For example, a Spanish wallet holder checking into a German hotel.
- PID cross-border trust: The hotel system resolves PID issuer trust via the APTITUDE WP2 trust framework, which federates national PID Trusted Issuer Lists. The hotel system does not need to maintain bilateral trust relationships with each Member State's PID issuer.
- EAA cross-border trust: The EAAs issued by IDnow are trusted by any RP that has access to the APTITUDE pilot TIL, regardless of the RP's Member State.
- Attribute normalisation: PID attributes (e.g., names, dates) follow the mandatory attribute set defined in CIR 2024/2977. However, transliteration differences and name ordering conventions may require the hotel system to implement fuzzy matching for the booking-to-PID name comparison (Phase 3).
