# 8. Trusted Issuer Lists

## 8.1 Overview

Trusted Issuer Lists (TILs) are the mechanism by which Relying Parties establish trust in credential issuers. In UC 10, two categories of TIL are relevant:

| TIL Category | Issuer(s) | Maintained By | Used By |
|--------------|-----------|---------------|---------|
| **PID Trusted Issuer List** | National PID issuers (one per Member State) | Each Member State government, federated via WP2 | Booking Platform, Hotel, Authorities — to validate PID issuer signatures |
| **EAA Trusted Issuer List** | IDnow (QTSP) | APTITUDE WP2 pilot registry | Booking Platform, Hotel, Door readers — to validate EAA issuer signatures |

---

## 8.2 IDnow's TIL Registration

IDnow will register as a trusted EAA issuer in the APTITUDE pilot TIL. The registration will include:

- **Issuer identifier:** IDnow's unique issuer DID or URI as used in the `iss` claim of all issued SD-JWT VCs.
- **Public key material:** IDnow's signing public key (or certificate) used to sign EAAs, enabling Relying Parties to validate credential signatures.
- **Supported credential types:** The three EAA types issued by IDnow in UC 10 (Booking Confirmation, Digital Room Key, Guest Summary).
- **QTSP status:** Reference to IDnow's qualified trust service provider status under eIDAS 2.0.

---

## 8.3 TIL Discovery and Resolution

Relying Parties resolve issuer trust at verification time as follows:

- **Online resolution (Phases 2, 3, 5):** The RP queries the TIL endpoint (provided by WP2) to retrieve IDnow's public key and validate the credential signature in real-time.
- **Offline resolution (Phase 4 — door reader):** The door reader pre-caches IDnow's public key from the TIL during periodic sync windows (see Section 3.4). At presentation time, the cached key is used for offline signature validation.

---

## 8.4 PID Issuer Trust Resolution

When the hotel system (or booking platform) receives a PID from a wallet, it validates the PID issuer's signature against the federated PID TIL provided by WP2. This federation allows RPs in one Member State to trust PID issuers from other Member States without bilateral agreements.

## 8.5 Wallet-Side Issuer Validation

Before storing an EAA received from IDnow, the EUDIW validates that IDnow is a registered issuer in the TIL. This prevents the wallet from storing credentials from unregistered or rogue issuers. The validation uses the same TIL endpoint as the RPs.
