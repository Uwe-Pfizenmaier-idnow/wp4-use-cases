# 6. PID (Person Identification Data)

## 6.1 PID Usage in UC 10

The PID is the foundational identity credential in UC 10. It is issued by the government of the user's Member State and stored in the EUDIW as a pre-condition (Phase 1). The PID is used at three points in the journey:

| Phase | PID Usage | Purpose |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Phase 2 — Booking** | PID presented to Booking Platform | Verify guest identity for booking; populate booking record with verified name |
| **Phase 3 — Check-in** | PID presented to Hotel | Verify guest identity; match against booking; satisfy national guest registration requirements |
| **Phase 5 — Authority reporting** | PID subset shared with authorities | Fulfil legal guest registration obligations (EU Reg. 2025/12, national transpositions) |

> **Phase 4 (door unlock):** The PID is **NOT** presented at the door reader. The Digital Room Key EAA is sufficient for room access. This is an explicit design decision to avoid unnecessary identity disclosure at every door interaction.

---

## 6.2 Required PID Attributes

Per CIR 2024/2977, the PID contains both mandatory and optional attributes. UC 10 requires the following:

### Mandatory PID Attributes Used

| Attribute | CIR Reference | Used In | Reason |
|-----------|---------------|---------|--------|
| `family_name` | Mandatory | Phases 2, 3, 5 | Booking name matching, guest registration, authority reporting |
| `given_name` | Mandatory | Phases 2, 3, 5 | Booking name matching, guest registration, authority reporting |
| `birth_date` | Mandatory | Phases 2, 3, 5 | Identity verification, age confirmation, authority reporting |

### Optional PID Attributes Required by UC 10

| Attribute | CIR Reference | Used In | Reason |
|-----------|---------------|---------|--------|
| `nationality` | Optional | Phases 2, 3, 5 | Required for authority reporting under EU Reg. 2025/12 and most national hotel registration laws |
| `document_number` | Optional | Phases 3, 5 | Required for police registration in several Member States (e.g., Spain, Italy, Portugal) |
| `place_of_birth` | Optional | Phase 5 | Required by some national hotel registration laws for authority reporting |

## 6.3 PID as Identity Anchor

The PID serves as the trust anchor for the entire UC 10 flow. The issuance of all three EAAs is predicated on successful PID verification at eIDAS **Level of Assurance High**. This means:

- The user has been identified by their national PID issuer using a high-assurance eID scheme.
- The PID credential is cryptographically bound to the wallet and cannot be transferred.
- IDnow (as QTSP) verifies the PID before issuing any EAA, ensuring that the EAAs inherit the PID's identity assurance level.

---

## 6.4 Cross-Border PID Acceptance

UC 10 accepts PIDs issued by any EU Member State. The hotel system (Relying Party) validates the PID issuer's signature against the PID Trusted Issuer List federated through the WP2 trust framework. No bilateral trust arrangements between the hotel and individual Member State PID issuers are required.

**Attribute normalisation considerations:**

- **Name ordering:** Some PIDs may present `family_name` before `given_name` or vice versa. The hotel system must handle both conventions.
- **Transliteration:** Names in non-Latin scripts are transliterated per ICAO standards (Doc 9303). The booking-to-PID name matching in Phase 3 must tolerate transliteration variations.
- **Date formats:** `birth_date` follows ISO 8601 as mandated by CIR 2024/2977.
